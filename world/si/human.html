Human




Scientific Name
Homo Sapiens
Native Planet
Earth
Average Size (Male;Female)
69 inches (175.26 cm); 64 inches (162.56 cm)
Average Lifespan
79 Earth years
Diet Type
Omnivorous

 The most common humanoids. They’re naturally inventive and creative to make up for their lack of having any natural magical or otherwise unique capabilities. Humans tend to be very social and inventive.

Habitat

  Each and every human is different, so the ideal habitat differs from person to person. However each human has around the same limits of their body. Every human is able to adapt to many different environments and can survive almost anywhere as long as there is oxygen, clean water, and a reliable food source.

Common Behaviors

 With every culture in human society comes different common behaviors and even within these cultures and between each human, these behaviors are different. Some common behaviors shared by almost every human are: singing, dancing, collecting objects, expressing oneself through clothing and appearance (dying hair, piercings, etc.), and talking with other humans (humans are very social).

Family Structure

  Within the different cultures of humans brings different familial structures. In many places these structures tend to be patriarchal while in other places there are matriarchal structures. However it is almost universal for all family structures to give more respect to those who are older such as grandparents or parents.

Life Stages:

  • Infant: ≤1 y/o

  • Toddler: ~2-3 y/o

  • Child: ~4-8 y/o

  • Puberty: ~9-13 y/o

  • Adolescent: ~14-18 y/o

  • Young adult: ~19-30 y/o

  • Middle aged: ~31-50 y/o

  • Senior: 51+ y/o

Language

 Humans have many different languages across the globe. However the most common languages are Mandarin Chinese, English, Hindustani, Spanish, and Arabic. Many of these languages also have dialects, due to their widespread plus influences from other regions. Humans are also able to pick up and learn languages that differ from their native tongues and do so often.

Diet

  Most humans are omnivorous. Some humans however can have a vulnerability to certain foods, causing allergies rendering them unable to eat certain foods such as gluten intolerance (unable to eat foods containing gluten such as breads) or nut allergies (unable to eat nuts). Some humans choose not to eat other animals, and some go as far as not eating products that come from other animals either! These are called vegetarians and vegans respectively. There are also humans who follow certain diets due to their spiritual beliefs, such as in Islam you may not eat pork.

Way of Dress

  Ways of dress vary from place to place all over the world, however it is generally accepted that it is inappropriate to walk in public without clothing and scandalous to wear only underwear.

Religion

 Humans have many different religions and spiritual beliefs, often affected by where they live and their family, however it is not unheard of for humans to change religions or choose not to affiliate with them. The most common religions are Christianity, Islam, and Hinduism, lacking religion (Atheism) has also been on the rise. For humans that choose to follow a religion they will sometimes go to a house of worship on a certain day every week or so, however some religions do not have this and some humans prefer not go to these places.
